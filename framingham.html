Framingham Ten-Year Coronary Heart Disease Risk

The calculator tool will determine your risk percentage, risk category, and "heart age" -- a metric that help you determine your heart health relative to your actual age. Generally, the younger your heart age the better your health.

If your heart age is close to your actual age (+/- 2.5 - 3.0 years) this is considered to be quite healthy.

A heart age around 5 years of your actual age can indicate a moderate risk for cardiovascular disease, while a heart age above 5 years of your actual age could indicate an issue and should be discusssed with a healthcare provider.

It should be noted that "heart age" is more of a conceptual gauge rather than a standard medical metric.

Note: A high or intermediate Framingham Risk Score is NOT a death sentence in today's world provided the individual is willing to commit to an overall healthy lifestyle (encompassing both physical and mental health).

As seen in the table below, your risk level in combination with other conditions is a reference point for your healthcare provider to help you develop an appropriate course of action based off of your individual background and needs.

Framingham Risk Score & Cardiovascular Disease Risk

An individual's Framingham Risk Score is used to determine an individual's risk of developing cardiovascular diseases within 10 years. Specifically, your scores predict the likelihood of developing a myocardial infraction (commonly referred to as a heart attack). These scores are useful for predicting coronary diseases in a 10 year time frame as well.

Framingham Risk Scores are most useful for adults over the age of 30, this calculator tool is designed to make predictions for any adult over the age of 30. Men above the age of 50 and women above the age of 60 are at a heightened risk for heart attacks. Generally, a patient with an intermediate or high Framingham Risk score will be asked to begin a statin regminet or similar medicinal treatment while improveing lifestyle factors and taking measures to improve overall well-being.

The good news is that lowering your Framingham Risk Score is a very attainable goal for most people since genetics play a relatively limited role in the genesis of cardivascular disease when compared to the impact of lifestyle factors. Numerous studies and anecdotal observation have shown that eating a well balanced diet high in vegetables and moderate amounts healthy fats in combination with a physically active lifestlye are vital in sustainabily lowering cardiovascular risk. Increasing evidence leads us to believe that a "Mediterranean Diet" consists of food patterns that are incredibly good for your cardiovasular and general health. Avoiding excess exposure to smoke and limiting or eliminating alcohol intake are instrumental in keeping your risk low.

Lifestyle driven ailments such as Type-II Diabetes, Insulin resistance, and high amounts of visceral fat are strongly correlated with increased cardiovascular risk, as an individual improves insulin resistance and looses visceral fat their risk factor for cardiovascular disease and it's drivers decreases significantly. The standard American diet and modern Western lifestyle can make overcoming these ailments and risk factors challenging at times, however with the right advice, dedication, and a growth-driven mindset, several individuals have worked to lower their risk scores significantly.

High Framingham Risk Score (FRS > 20%) Consider treatment for anyone at this risk level irrespective of lifestyle factors and prexisting conditions
